Introduction

Welcome to EasySOA Core & Light!

First time user? Take the tour!

For the Business user

Note: Before using this demo, please set your browser's proxy to localhost:8081 and remove any proxy exceptions (unless you're using our virtual machine). If you're asked for a user/password, just type Administrator for both.

Step 1
Discover services in your intranet

More on discovery by browsingFor tests purposes, this demo packs a mockup intranet of a company named Pure Air Flowers. Let's browse it using the Service discovery. From there, as you visit pages, you may see a service appearing in the left menu, meaning that EasySOA just found it. You're then two clicks away to register this service: just select "PureAirFlowers Orders", then click "Save Service", and you're done!

Step 2
Explore all services

More on the service registryNow, let's have a look to our registered service, that you will find in the Service registry. Once you're logged in, navigate to Workspaces > Services. You will find here the service you just registered. If you click on it, all metadata are now here, business and technical (look for a link on the right to see "WSDL" details). You may override most of these metadata, and fill new ones if you want to document your service.

There are various ways to browse your services: other than the default navigation tree, there is of course a search form, but also what is called "virtual navigations". These can be accessed from TODO

Step 3
Call a service

Now you can use this service to get business data or do a business operation! While you're on the service registry, notice the "Call it!" button on your service page. Click it. The interface you now see has been generated from the registered definition of the service. Here you can enter the input parameters, and start a request. Is the service answering "10"? It's working!

This call is actually made in an SOA sandbox that proxies, monitors and protects actual services. In this case, it prevents the user from using them excessively. You can test it by calling it repeatedly : the error comes once you've called the service more than 10 times in 2 minutes. Now our calls are blocked for the remaining 2 minutes! We are even planning to improve this, so that if you call a service more than 10 times a second, EasySOA electrocutes you.

Conclusion

For now, EasySOA is a demo provided with all imaginable disclaimers... But later, lots of features will be added, from collaborative feature and true proxy generation to an all-in-one architecture. For more advanced features, including services monitoring and architecture files import, please see the second demo scenario.

For Developers & the IT staff

This scenario is based on another demo SOA, called "Smart Travel". This time, we will take the role of a developer who wants to map the SOA contents.

Step 1
Import the SOA architecture

More on SCA importFirst, we are going to import the whole SOA architecture into the service registry. In order to do that, a feature called "SCA import" is able to analyze the definition of the SOA components. From the service registry, create a new application by going to the "Service registry" document and choosing "New Application Implementation" ; create it with "Smart Travel" as the title and "http://localhost" for the URL. Then, import the SCA file through the "SCA data import" page, available from the top menu. Choose the file located in "exemples/smart-travel-mock-services.composite" (in your EasySOA folder), then select the newly created application for the import, and go!

Step 2
Explore the services

You can now browse through various services in the left menu. Only two of them are in the "Smart Travel" application, since others are external services, which are only referenced from your application. All the imported data can be useful to help developers and architects, even more once documentation files are attached to the services (you can do it by editing any document).

Now, what if a member of the IT staff wants to know what really happens on the network?

Step 3
SOA Monitoring

More on discovery by monitoringIn EasySOA, monitoring can be used both to discover new services, and simply to monitor the information system. You will eventually have the possibility to set up monitoring anywhere, by configuring clients to use a specific proxy. For now, you can test this feature by using EasySOA Light, since monitoring is configured on it by default. From Nuxeo, browse to the "Create Summary" service, and click the "Call It!" button: all requests you will send from there will be intercepted by our proxy, that will then send information about this service to Nuxeo.

Step 4
Explore the service Pt. 2: data correlation

Notice that thanks to monitoring and SCA import, we get two different ways to find the same services, gathering complementary information. If you add the discovery by browsing tool (see demo scenario #1), they form an interesting way to gather transversal data regarding the information system. The correlation between these discovery methods is represented by three icons shown on every document page: . Browse to the "Create Summary" service to see how these icons are enabled for the browsing and monitoring discovery. There, you will also see that the "call count" property has been increased!

Step 5
The dashboard

More on the dashboardSince we are storing in EasySOA how many time each service has been called, why not make a dashboard to provide all these information on a synthetic page? The dashboard, available from the top menu, allows you to organize various widgets on the same page. One of those widgets is called "EasySOA Stats", add it to see a list of services showing how many called has been made on each one.

Conclusion

These two demos show you various ways to use and interact with EasySOA and its transverse model. Since users of various profiles will be able to use the solution, why not make them work together, thanks to collaborative features? What if the business user could directly send requests to the developers by "commenting" a service? The ambitions of EasySOA go even further that what you just saw. For more information, see the temporary EasySOA website. Thanks for testing EasySOA!
