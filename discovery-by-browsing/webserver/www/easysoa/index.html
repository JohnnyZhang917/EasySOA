Introduction

Welcome to EasySOA Core & Light!

First time user? Take the tour!

Note: Before using this demo, please set your browser's proxy to localhost:8081 and remove any proxy exceptions (unnecessary if you're using our virtual machine). If you're asked for a user/password, just type Administrator for both.

Step 1
Discover services in your intranet

For tests purposes, this demo packs a mockup intranet of a company named Pure Aire Flowers. Let's browse it using the Service discovery. From there, as you browse, you may see a service appearing in the left menu, meaning that EasySOA just found it. You're then two clicks away to register this service : just select it, then click "Save Service", and your done!

Step 2
Explore all services

Now, let's have a look to our registered service, that you will find in the Service registry. Once you're logged in, navigate to Workspaces > Services. You will fond here the service you just registered. If you click on it, all metadata are now here, business and technical (look for a link on the right to see "WSDL" details). You may override most of these metadata, and fill new ones if you want to document your service.

Step 3
Call a service

Now you can use this service to get business data or do a business operation! While you're on the service registry, notice the "Call it!" button on your service page. Click it. The interface you now see has been generated from the registered definition of the service. Here you can enter the input parameters, and start a request. Is the service answering "10"? It's working!

This call is actually made through a proxy layer that prevents from excessive use. You can test it by calling it repeatedly : the error comes once you've called the service more than 10 times in 2 minutes. Now our calls are blocked for the remaining 2 minutes! We are even planning to improve this, so that if you call a service more than 10 times a second, EasySOA electrocutes you.

Conclusion

For now, EasySOA is a demo provided with all imaginable disclaimers... But later, lots of features will be added, from true proxy generation to an all-in-one architecture. Our next goals also include REST services compatibility, a cloud-based solution, services monitoring...
